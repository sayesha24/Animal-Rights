Animal Rights in India

What do you say when you are in pain?
Ouch perhaps?
but have you ever thought about what would these voiceless animals say when they are in pain?

But hopefully, the Indian government has established certain laws and acts that prevent animal cruelty.

Animal Rights and Laws with Consequences:


1. Each citizen holds a duty of being compassionate and empathise with all the living creatures.

2. To kill any animal including stray animals is a punishable offence.

3. You can land in a prison up to 3 months if a pet is abandoned regardless of the reason.

4. No animal, including chickens can be slaughtered in any place other than a slaughterhouse.

5. Sick or pregnant animals shall not be slaughtered.

6. Stray dogs that have been operated for birth control cannot be captured or relocated by anybody including any authority.

7. Not providing a pet with the elements important for survival including access to food, water and shelter
and keeping the pet chained for hours can lead to imprisonment.

8. Monkeys cannot be owned.

9. Bears, monkeys, tigers, panthers, lions and bulls are prohibited from being trained
and used for entertainment purposes, either in circuses or streets.

10. Animal sacrifice is illegal.

11. Holding or organising an animal fight is a cognizable offence.

12. Experimentation and cosmetics test on animals is banned.

13. Teasing, feeding or disturbing the animals in a zoo and littering the zoo premises is an offence punishable
by a fine of Rs. 25000 or imprisonment of up to three years or both.

14. Trapping, poisoning or baiting of any animal leads to a fine or an imprisonment up to 7 years.

15. Destroying the eggs or the nests of birds and reptiles or chopping a tree with the nests of birds and
reptiles has a fine of 25000 rupees and imprisonment of up to 7 years.

16. Carrying animals (in a vehicle) wherein they are in discomfort or in a position which is causing pain or
suffering is a punishable offence.







Such laws created by the Indian government under various acts can truly help strengthen and improve the relationship
between the nature/ environment and the mankind.

How our Future Would Look like if Animal Cruelty Continued


If animal cruelty continues in any form then,
we will soon see a world buzzing with the city sounds rather than the chirping of the birds,
a trail of cars and buildings instead of the lush trees
and a handful of insects occupying the confined forests with half cut trees.

Hence, these rights will aid the animals to have a compartively easier survival in this daunting world and make us,
the responsible citizens of India realise our duty towards these innocent and vulnerable species.
